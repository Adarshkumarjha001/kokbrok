The People

Borok People & Tripura

Language Overview

Kokborok, also known as Tripuri, is a Tibeto-Burman language of the Sino-Tibetan family, spoken primarily by the Borok people in the Indian state of Tripura and parts of Bangladesh and Myanmar. With approximately one million native speakers, it is one of the two official languages of Tripura.

The Borok people have a rich oral tradition — folk songs (Hojagiri dance), epic poetry, and ritual chants passed down through generations. The language stands as a living testament to the resilience and cultural identity of the Tripuri community.

Milestones

Language Timeline

Ancient Oral Era

Living Oral Tradition

Kokborok thrives as a rich oral language. Epic folklore, ancestral songs (Hojagiri rituals), and ceremonial chants preserve Borok cosmology and identity across generations — long before any formal writing system.

Colonial Era — 1800s

First Documentation

British colonial scholars and early missionaries begin documenting Kokborok. The first written records appear using the Bengali script. The language begins to be studied linguistically by outside researchers.

1972

Statehood & Recognition

Tripura achieves full statehood. Kokborok gains increasing political visibility. Movements for official recognition of the language in education and governance gain momentum among the Borok community.

1979

Official Language Status

Kokborok is declared one of the official languages of Tripura alongside Bengali. This landmark recognition paves the way for Kokborok-medium schools and state publications.

2000s

Digital Push

Keyboard layouts, Unicode support, and online dictionaries begin to emerge for Kokborok. However, NLP resources remain extremely scarce, making computational language processing nearly impossible for researchers.

2024 — Present

AI for Kokborok

A fine-tuned XLM-RoBERTa model for Kokborok POS tagging — the first publicly available transformer for this language — opens the door to machine translation, sentiment analysis, and beyond.

Linguistics

Language Features

🗂️

Language Family

Sino-Tibetan → Tibeto-Burman branch. Closest relatives include other Tripuri dialects, Reang (Bru), and Northeast Indian languages.

✍️

Writing Systems

Historically written in Bengali script. A Roman-script (Koloma) romanization system was developed for wider digital accessibility and educational use.

🔤

Grammar Structure

Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) word order, agglutinative morphology, and a rich postposition system — entirely distinct from Indo-Aryan neighbouring languages.
